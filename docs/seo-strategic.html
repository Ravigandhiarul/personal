MEDIUM-TERM #3: Site Speed Optimization

Priority: üü† HIGH - Months 1-2

Timeline: 4-6 weeks

Impact: 5-10% conversion rate improvement + better rankings

Why This Matters Right Now

Your traffic is declining -17.69% month-over-month. That's alarming. While there are likely multiple factors, site speed is both a ranking factor (affects how much traffic you get) and a conversion factor (affects what you do with the traffic you have).

Google research shows 53% of mobile users abandon pages that take longer than 3 seconds to load. Every 0.1-second improvement = 1-2% conversion increase.

For Sessions with 273.2K monthly visits, speed directly impacts revenue.

What I Discovered Through Technical Audits

To validate my speed concerns, I ran two audits:

1. W3C Link Checker Audit (Technical)

I ran sessions.ca through W3C Link Checker and found 16 instances of missing trailing slashes causing 301 redirects:

Examples:

  • /rosedale-cannabis-menu ‚Üí 301 redirects to ‚Üí /rosedale-cannabis-menu/
  • /rosedale-cannabis-menu/brands ‚Üí 301 redirects to ‚Üí /rosedale-cannabis-menu/brands/

Impact: Each redirect adds 100-300ms latency. With 16 redirect chains, that's 1.6-4.8 seconds of avoidable delay.

I also found a broken accessibility anchor (the "Skip to Content" link points to #content which doesn't exist on the page). This breaks keyboard navigation for screen readers‚Äîan AODA compliance issue.

2. Google PageSpeed Insights (Benchmark)

I ran the homepage and several location pages through PageSpeed Insights:

  • Mobile scores: 45-60 (needs improvement)
  • Desktop scores: 65-75 (fair)
  • Largest Contentful Paint (LCP): 3.5-4.2 seconds (should be <2.5s)

My Implementation Plan (3 Phases)

As your Digital Marketing Manager, I would approach speed optimization systematically:

Phase 1: Emergency Fixes (Days 1-2) - $600 investment

Fix the issues I found in the W3C audit:

1. Trailing slash redirects:

  • Use WordPress "Better Search Replace" plugin
  • Update all internal links to include trailing slashes
  • Timeline: 2 hours
  • Cost: $500 (developer time if needed, or I can do it)
  • Impact: 0.3-0.5s faster page loads

2. Broken accessibility anchor:

  • Add id="content" to main content container
  • Code: <main id="content"> or <div class="content" id="content">
  • Timeline: 15 minutes
  • Cost: $100
  • Impact: AODA compliance restored

Phase 2: Caching & Optimization (Weeks 1-2) - $2,000 investment

Install and configure WP Rocket caching plugin ($59/year):

WP Rocket Settings I'd Enable:

‚Üí Caching:
  ‚úÖ Enable mobile caching
  ‚úÖ Cache lifespan: 10 hours

‚Üí File Optimization:
  ‚úÖ Minify CSS/JS files
  ‚úÖ Defer JavaScript loading
  ‚úÖ Optimize CSS delivery

‚Üí Media:
  ‚úÖ Lazy load images
  ‚úÖ Lazy load iframes/videos

‚Üí Preload:
  ‚úÖ Preload cache (crawls site, generates cache)

Expected improvement: 40-60% faster repeat visits

I'd also install ShortPixel for image optimization:

  • Bulk optimize existing media library
  • Auto-optimize new uploads
  • Convert to WebP format (30-50% smaller than JPEG)

Expected improvement: 30-50% reduction in page weight

Phase 3: Hosting Assessment (Month 2) - $0-3,000 investment

After implementing Phase 1-2 optimizations, I would benchmark performance again. If we're still not hitting targets (<2.5s mobile LCP), the hosting infrastructure may be the bottleneck.

Current setup assessment needed:

  • Are you on shared hosting? (likely limiting with 273.2K monthly visits)
  • Server response time acceptable? (should be <200ms)
  • PHP version up to date? (7.4+ required for performance)

If hosting upgrade needed:

  • Options: WP Engine, Kinsta, SiteGround (performance tiers)
  • Cost: $30-250/month depending on tier
  • Expected improvement: 20-40% faster server response time

Decision point: I'd only recommend hosting upgrade if Phase 1-2 don't achieve targets. No point spending money unnecessarily.

STRATEGIC #4: Launch Educational Content Hub

Priority: üü° MEDIUM - Months 3-6

Timeline: 6 months (for foundation)

Impact: Long-term traffic growth + brand authority

Why Content Marketing Matters for Sessions

Here's the reality: the Federal Cannabis Act prohibits Google Ads and Meta advertising. You can't run paid ads. SEO is your ONLY digital acquisition channel.

But most cannabis retailers focus only on transactional SEO (product pages, location pages). They're competing for high-intent, high-competition keywords like "cannabis store [city]."

Opportunity: Capture customers earlier in the journey with informational content.

Someone searching "how to choose cannabis edibles" isn't ready to buy today. But if Sessions teaches them, when they're ready to buy, who do they think of? The brand that helped them learn.

Competitive Context

I researched your top competitors' content strategies:

Tokyo Smoke:

  • Active blog with monthly posts
  • "Cannabis 101" education hub
  • Strain profiles and product guides
  • 50+ published articles (estimate)

Canna Cabana:

  • Minimal blog activity
  • Focus on product pages only

Sessions:

  • No blog
  • Zero content marketing in 2024

The gap: Tokyo Smoke is capturing informational searches. You're not. But Canna Cabana isn't either, so there's opportunity.

My Content Strategy (6-Month Foundation)

As your Digital Marketing Manager, I would launch a blog focused on beginner-friendly education:

Phase 1: Foundational Content (Months 3-4)

Target: 8 posts in 2 months (2 per month)

Priority topics:

  1. "Beginner's Guide to Cannabis Formats: Flower, Pre-rolls & Edibles"
    • Target keyword: "cannabis formats for beginners"
    • Search volume: 2,400/month
    • Difficulty: Medium
    • Internal links: Product category pages
  2. "How to Choose the Right THC Dosage"
    • Target keyword: "THC dosage guide"
    • Search volume: 3,600/month
    • Difficulty: Medium
  3. "Understanding Terpenes: Beyond THC and CBD"
    • Target keyword: "what are terpenes"
    • Search volume: 14,800/month
    • Difficulty: High (but foundational topic)
  4. "Safe Cannabis Storage Tips"
    • Target keyword: "how to store cannabis"
    • Search volume: 1,900/month
    • Difficulty: Low (quick win)
  5. "Ontario Cannabis Laws Explained"
    • Target keyword: "Ontario cannabis laws"
    • Search volume: 1,600/month
    • Difficulty: Low

  6. "Indica vs. Sativa: What's the Difference?"
    • Target keyword: "indica vs sativa"

    • Difficulty: Very high (but must-have topic)
  7. 7-8. Two more beginner topics based on keyword research

This is where the franchise model becomes an advantage:

  • Builds local SEO signals (community involvement, local backlinks)
  • Personal connection with customers

Target: 4 localized posts (1 every 2 weeks)

Content SEO Optimization (My Process)

For every post I'd create, I'd follow this checklist:

Technical SEO:

  • ‚úÖ Target keyword in title (front-loaded)
  • ‚úÖ Target keyword in URL slug (/blog/cannabis-formats-guide/)
  • ‚úÖ Target keyword in H1
  • ‚úÖ Natural keyword usage (3-5 times in content)
  • ‚úÖ Internal links: 3-5 per post (to products, locations, other posts)
  • ‚úÖ External links: 2-3 to authoritative sources (Health Canada, research)
  • ‚úÖ Featured image optimized (<100KB, WebP, descriptive alt text)
  • ‚úÖ Meta description: 150-160 characters

  • Conversational tone (beginner-friendly, not intimidating)
  • Actionable takeaways (not just information)
  • Visual elements (images, comparison tables)

Why I'm Confident This Works
