EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Challenge I Uncovered

When I started researching Sessions Cannabis, I was immediately struck by the competitive reality: 1,800+ licensed retailers in Ontario, and the Federal Cannabis Act prohibits Google Ads and Meta advertising. This means local SEO isn't just important—it's literally the only digital acquisition channel you have.

Your website gets 273.2K monthly visits, but traffic is declining -17.69% month-over-month. That's a red flag that demands attention.

So I dug deeper. I ran technical audits, analyzed competitors, and cross-referenced multiple data sources to understand what's causing the decline and—more importantly—what can be fixed.

What I Found

The short version: There are critical execution gaps costing Sessions significant revenue, but they're fixable. Most are technical issues that compound across your 45+ location network.

Three Issues Stood Out Immediately

1
Zero schema markup across all locations (0% implementation)
  • Your competitors Tokyo Smoke and Canna Cabana: 100% implementation
  • Impact: Lower visibility in "near me" searches, no rich snippets in search results
  • This affects all 45+ locations simultaneously
2
NAP (Name, Address, Phone) inconsistencies across platforms
  • I found examples: Yelp showing "4700 Dufferin Avenue" when it should be "Street"
  • Four different naming formats being used across locations
  • Google gets confused about which entity is which → rankings suffer
3
Weak review management (minimal response rate)
  • Only found ONE official response on Weedmaps across all locations
  • Industry standard: 80% response rate. Sessions: ~5-10%
  • This is both a ranking factor AND trust signal for customers

My Recommendations Are Prioritized by Impact vs. Effort

I've organized my findings into Quick Wins (Days 1-30), Medium-Term Projects (Months 2-3), and Strategic Initiatives (Months 4-6). This isn't a wish list—it's an execution roadmap showing what I'd tackle first as your Digital Marketing Manager.

Quick Wins Deliver Immediate ROI
  • Implement LocalBusiness schema (1-2 weeks, affects all locations)
  • Optimize title tags for local keywords (1-2 weeks, increases click-through rate)
  • Fix W3C technical issues (Days 1-2, improves site speed)
Medium-Term Projects Build Foundation
  • Site speed optimization (1-2 months, reduces bounce rate)
  • Mobile UX improvements (ongoing, affects 60-70% of traffic)
Strategic Initiatives Position for Growth
  • Educational content hub (3-6 months, captures informational searches)
  • City-specific landing pages (3-6 months, targets high-volume keywords)

The Franchise Model Context

One thing I noticed throughout my analysis: there's significant performance variance across locations. Some locations are clearly optimized (complete Google Business Profiles, responding to reviews, good photos). Others have major gaps.

This tells me two things:

  1. Success is achievable (top-performing locations prove it)
  2. Centralized oversight is needed (bottom-performing locations show what happens without it)

As your Digital Marketing Manager, I would focus on creating systems that scale—standardized templates, automation where possible, and franchisee training for local execution. The goal isn't to control everything centrally, but to ensure baseline standards while allowing local flexibility.
