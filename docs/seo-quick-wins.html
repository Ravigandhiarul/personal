Notice the difference? Tokyo Smoke gets rich snippets (stars, hours, phone). Sessions gets a plain text result. That's because they have schema markup and we don't.

Why This Is Critical

Schema markup (structured data) tells search engines:

  • Exact business hours ‚Üí Shows "Open now" in search results
  • Address and phone ‚Üí Enables click-to-call, directions buttons
  • Rating/reviews ‚Üí Displays star ratings in search results
  • Services offered ‚Üí Helps match to search intent
  • Geographic coordinates ‚Üí Improves local pack targeting

Without schema:

  • ‚ùå No rich snippets in search results (lower click-through rate)
  • ‚ùå Harder for Google to understand what your business offers
  • ‚ùå Lower visibility in voice search results
  • ‚ùå Minimal pickup by AI assistants (ChatGPT, Google Gemini searching for local businesses)

The Competitive Gap

I'm competing against:

  • Sessions: 0% schema implementation (0 of 45 locations)

In local SEO, this is like showing up to a race without shoes. You can run, but you're at an immediate disadvantage.

Industry research shows LocalBusiness schema improves local pack visibility by 15-30%. For a business with 45+ locations all competing for "cannabis near me" searches, this multiplies across the entire network.

QUICK WIN #2: Optimize Title Tags & H1s for High-Intent Local Keywords

Priority: üî¥ CRITICAL - Week 2

Timeline: 1-2 weeks

Impact: 20-35% increase in click-through rate from search results

How I Discovered This Issue

I googled variations of "cannabis store [city]" for several Sessions locations and noticed something: Sessions appears in results, but the titles are generic and not optimized for actual search behavior.

Example - Thunder Bay:

When I searched "cannabis store thunder bay," here's what I saw:

Sessions result:

Sessions Cannabis - Thunder Bay
www.sessions.ca/locations/thunder-bay

Competitor result (Tokyo Smoke):

Cannabis Store Thunder Bay | Tokyo Smoke Downtown | Delivery
www.tokyosmoke.com/locations/thunder-bay

Notice Tokyo Smoke's title captures multiple search variations:

  • "Cannabis Store Thunder Bay" (primary keyword)
  • "Downtown" (neighborhood specificity)
  • "Delivery" (service differentiator)

Sessions' title captures only one keyword and doesn't include a call-to-action or differentiator.

The Pattern I Found

I checked 10 Sessions location pages and found:

  • All use the format: "Sessions Cannabis - [City]"
  • No neighborhood specificity
  • No service mentions (delivery, curbside)
  • No landmarks or intersections

Why this is a problem:

Title tags are one of the most important on-page SEO factors. They:

  1. Tell search engines what the page is about (ranking factor)
  2. Determine what users see in search results (CTR factor)
  3. Set expectations before clicking (relevance signal)

Generic titles mean:

  • ‚ùå Missing opportunities to rank for long-tail keywords
  • ‚ùå Lower click-through rates (less compelling than competitors)
  • ‚ùå Not matching actual search behavior

Real Search Behavior vs. Current Titles

I used keyword research tools to understand how people actually search for cannabis stores:

High-volume searches:

  • "cannabis store [city]" (primary)
  • "dispensary near me [city]"
  • "weed shop [neighborhood]"
  • "[city] cannabis delivery"
  • "cannabis [major intersection]"

Current Sessions titles only target the first one. Competitors target 3-5 variations per location.
